Issuance of Readmission Card After an Absence From Class



  1. Readmission card for absences shall be issued to a student after his/her first absence. Student's and adviser's copies of the form can be obtained from the school/college office.
  2. The student is required to present an excuse letter and appropriate document(s) such as:
    1. Medical Certificate in case of illness/accidents,
    2. Death Certificate in case of death of an immediate relative.
  3. First and second absences may be handled by the student's section adviser, provided absence has not been for 3 consecutive days.
  4. Third absence/absences for 3 consecutive days or more noted in the student's and adviser's copies must be referred to the Dean's office.
  5. The student may have his/her readmission card processed by his/her section adviser during their class or at the adviser's consultation time and day or at some other agreed time and day. Cases referred to the Dean's office will be attended to as scheduled.
  6. All the concerned teachers must sign the card within one week; thereafter, the student must return his/her readmission card to the section adviser. At the end of the semester, the section adviser must submit the card to the Dean's office for inclusion in the student's record folder/envelope.
  7. A student may be considered unofficially dropped after an absence of:
    1. 10.5 hours from a 3‐unit class;
    2. 7 hours from a 2‐unit class; and
    3. 3 hours from a 1‐unit class; or
    4. 20% or more of the required total semestral attendance for the subject.
